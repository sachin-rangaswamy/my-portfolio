## Molecules that switch on light

A **photoacid** is a molecule whose acidity jumps by many orders of magnitude when it
absorbs a photon. That makes it a light-controlled proton source — a beautiful tool for
photochemistry and catalysis, if you understand *why* the excited state is so much more
acidic.

## The quantum-chemical view

At the **Centre for Theoretical Chemistry, Ruhr University Bochum**, I used advanced
DFT and **post-Hartree–Fock** methods to compute ground- and excited-state electronic
structure of photoacids, investigating how excitation redistributes electron density
and destabilizes the acidic proton.

This project sits at the very bottom of the scale bridge — pure quantum mechanics —
and it shaped how I think about every scale above: all materials behaviour is, in the
end, electron redistribution.
