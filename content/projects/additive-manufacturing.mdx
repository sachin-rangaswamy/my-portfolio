## The challenge

**Directed energy deposition (DED)** builds parts layer by layer with a moving melt
pool. The result is a material whose microstructure varies point by point: rapid
solidification, repeated reheating, steep gradients. Conventional "one microstructure
per alloy" thinking breaks down completely.

## The scale-bridging pipeline

This is integrated computational materials engineering (ICME) in its purest form:

1. **Process → temperature field.** The moving heat source defines local thermal
   histories throughout the part.
2. **Temperature → thermodynamics.** CALPHAD calculations predict phase constitution
   along each thermal path — solidification sequences, phase fractions and
   transformation temperatures in multicomponent stainless steels.
3. **Thermodynamics → kinetics.** Diffusion-controlled transformation and precipitation
   models add the time axis that rapid AM processing makes so critical.
4. **Microstructure → properties.** The predicted local microstructure state maps to
   local mechanical behaviour across the component.

## Why it matters

If local properties can be predicted from process parameters, additive manufacturing
stops being a qualification nightmare and becomes a design tool: microstructure by
prescription, not by accident.
