## The problem

Every Li-ion cell dies first at its interfaces. Cathode particles react with the
electrolyte, surfaces reconstruct, impedance grows. Ultra-thin protective coatings —
often deposited by **atomic layer deposition (ALD)** — can suppress this degradation,
but the design space of coating chemistries is enormous.

## What I did

At **Volkswagen AG** (internship 2023–24, master's thesis 2024–25) I worked on:

- **DFT simulations of cathode coating materials**, computing stability, electronic
  structure and Li-transport properties of candidate coatings.
- **Ab-initio optimization of cathode components** in the Li–V–O system.
- **Simulation of ALD coatings** with theoretical-chemistry methods to understand
  electrode interface effects.
- **Machine-learning screening** to accelerate the discovery of new battery materials,
  targeting improved energy efficiency and stability.

## The multiscale connection

This project lives at the bottom of the scale bridge: quantum-mechanical interface
chemistry, propagated upward into statements an engineer can act on — which coating,
how thick, deposited how.
