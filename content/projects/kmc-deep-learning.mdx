## The bottleneck

**Kinetic Monte Carlo (KMC)** is the method of choice when the interesting physics
happens through rare events — diffusion hops, adsorption, reaction steps. Its weakness
is intrinsic: events are simulated one at a time, in sequence. Long-time, large-system
simulations become prohibitively expensive.

## The idea

During my internship at the **Bavarian Center for Battery Technology (BayBatt)** in
Bayreuth, I worked on *autoregressive deep learning* as an accelerator: train a network
on KMC trajectories so that it learns the distribution of state evolutions, then let it
propagate the system forward in large strides — the way a language model predicts the
next token, but for atomic configurations.

## Outlook

Surrogate dynamics is one of the most promising shortcuts in the multiscale toolbox:
keep the physics of the fine scale, but pay only a fraction of its cost when bridging
to engineering-relevant timescales.
